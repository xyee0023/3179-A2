Global AI Index & Job Market Impact

This visualization explores how countries are performing in the AI race, and the risks certain job titles face in the next decade due to AI automation. It highlights AI indicators, company adoption levels, and potential disruptions to the workforce.

Global AI Landscape

The global AI landscape is constantly evolving, with some countries leading the charge while others are slowly catching up. This map illustrates the AI scores (an index used to benchmark nations on their level of investment, innovation, and implementation of artificial intelligence) for various countries, categorized into five clusters: Waking up, Rising stars, Nascent, Traditional champions, and Power players. These clusters reflect the overall AI readiness and development level of each country, from those just beginning their AI journey to those leading the world. The AI Index score helps governments and policymakers assess their nation's position in the AI race.

AI Strength Comparison

Not all countries are equal when it comes to AI capabilities. This radar chart allows you to compare two countries based on seven critical AI factors: talent, research, development, AI infrastructure, Commercial, Government strategy, and Operating environment. Each of these factors plays a vital role in a country’s AI growth and competitiveness.

AI Adoption Across Industries

As AI technology advances, its adoption is spreading across various industries, transforming the way businesses operate. This grouped bar chart illustrates the level of AI adoption in different industries—categorized by high, medium, and low levels of implementation. While some sectors, such as Technology and Manufactoring, are quick to adopt AI due to their data-driven nature, other industries may be slower to embrace it. The chart provides insights into which industries are leading the AI revolution and which are still catching up.

AI Adoption vs Company Size and Salary

This bubble plot explores the relationship between the average salary offered by companies, their size (small, medium, or large), and the level of AI adoption within the company. Larger bubbles represent companies with higher AI adoption levels, while the position of each bubble reflects the company’s size and the average salary offered. This visualization helps highlight trends in how AI adoption correlates with company size and salary levels.

Jobs at Risk of Automation

The rise of AI doesn’t just bring opportunities—it also poses risks, particularly for jobs that are easily automated. This word cloud highlights job titles that are most at risk of being automated within the next decade. Larger words represent higher risks of automation. Roles such as Cybersecurity Analyst, Sales manager, and AI Researcher may face significant disruptions. This visualization underscores the importance of reskilling and upskilling the workforce to adapt to the evolving job landscape in the AI era.

Conclusion

As AI continues to advance, its impact on industries and the workforce becomes increasingly significant. From global AI leaders driving innovation to companies adopting AI technologies at different levels, the shift towards automation is reshaping the job market. While AI brings new opportunities, it also poses challenges, particularly for jobs at high risk of automation. Preparing the workforce through reskilling and upskilling will be crucial in ensuring a balanced, AI-driven future.
